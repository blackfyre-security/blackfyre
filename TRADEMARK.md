# Blackfyre Trademark Policy

"Blackfyre", the Blackfyre flame-shield logo, and the blackfyre.tech name are
trademarks of the project owner (Giridhar Kannabiran). The Apache-2.0 license
covers the **source code**; it does not grant rights to these marks.

The point of this policy is user protection, not code restriction: anyone
running something called "Blackfyre" should be getting the genuine project,
and no one should be misled into thinking a third-party product, service, or
event is run or endorsed by this project when it isn't. We want the marks
used — accurately.

## The marks

- The word mark **"Blackfyre"** (any capitalization or spacing)
- The **Blackfyre logo** (`website/public/brand/`)
- The **blackfyre.tech** name

## You may, without asking

- **Build, run, modify, and redistribute the code** under Apache-2.0 —
  nothing here limits those license rights.
- **Nominative use**: refer to the project by name to identify it —
  in articles, blog posts, books, talks, tutorials, package descriptions,
  and comparisons ("X is faster than Blackfyre" is fine if true).
- **Truthful statements of relationship**: "based on Blackfyre",
  "compatible with Blackfyre", "a fork of Blackfyre", "runs on Blackfyre" —
  as long as the statement is accurate and doesn't imply endorsement.
- **Unmodified redistribution**: distribute unmodified official releases
  under the Blackfyre name (e.g. a package repository), clearly pointing at
  this project as the source.
- **Link to the project** using the name or unmodified logo as a hyperlink to
  github.com/blackfyre-security/blackfyre or blackfyre.tech.
- **Personal, non-commercial items**: stickers or shirts for yourself, your
  team, or your meetup — not for sale.
- **Community activity**: user groups, meetups, and study groups using the
  name descriptively ("Chennai Blackfyre Users"), provided they're
  non-commercial, don't use the logo as their own branding, and make clear
  they are community-run, not official.

Usage guidance: use the mark as an adjective with the project's capitalization
("the Blackfyre platform"), don't abbreviate, hyphenate, pluralize, or merge
it into your own product name or logo, and don't restyle the logo (colors,
proportions, added elements).

## You may not, without written permission

- Offer a **product or service** (including a hosted/managed offering) under
  the name "Blackfyre" or a confusingly similar name or logo.
- **Forks must rename.** Modified versions may not ship as "Blackfyre" —
  pick your own name and say "a fork of Blackfyre" instead. (This mirrors
  the practice of Fedora, Node.js, and other open-source projects.)
- Register or use **domain names, social-media handles, or package
  namespaces** containing "blackfyre" in a way likely to confuse people about
  who runs them.
- Use the marks in your **company name, product name, or logo**, or in
  **paid advertising** as if your offering were the project.
- Sell **merchandise** bearing the marks.
- Claim **certification, partnership, or endorsement** ("official",
  "certified", "partner") that hasn't been granted in writing.
- Apply to **register** the marks (or confusingly similar ones) as your own
  trademarks anywhere.
